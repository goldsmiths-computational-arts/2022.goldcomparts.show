Jinyuan Li

One to Thousands of Worlds

Jinyuan Li is an artist and explorer with the background of graphic design and computational art. Her works specialize in the synesthesia of multiple perceptions and body movements, and involve from video, illustration to generative sound art, interactive performance, virtual reality and other digital or computational expressions. Her recent practices include immersive experience with other nonhuman perspectives, generative sound with neuromuscular and machine learning, etc.. Her works explore the combination between art and technology, and try to bring art and aesthetics into more aspects of our real life.

A VR project to let people embodied experience with other creatures' worlds, knowing that our human world is only one of the thousands worlds.

“One to Thousands of worlds” shows different worlds from our human’s. The frog experiment by Humberto Maturana and Francisco Varela showed the way of experiencing the world depended on our biological structures. So the world we know is not the only world but one of the thousands of worlds. Under the background of the environmental crisis and the self-defuturing society, we gradually know that our human and surrounding environment are interrelationship and symbiosis. We are not the center or the only subject in the world. In my project I want to change our usual human perspective into different ones like mantis shrimp, dragonfly, etc.. I use VR technology, imitate different animals’ worlds to let people have a embodiment experience through the combination of multiple perceptions and body movements, generate a feeling of close to nature and other nonhumans' lives, and also rethink about the relationship between our existence with surroundings in the posthuman age.
