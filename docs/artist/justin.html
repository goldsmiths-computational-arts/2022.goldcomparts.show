Justin Kuhn

Mercedonius

Justin is a transdisciplinary artist who uses code, sound, and pixels to make interactive, audiovisual works. They are currently pursuing an MA in Computational Arts at Goldsmiths, University of London. Since 2018, Justin has exhibited work using sonification, spatial audio, websites, saxophone, and live code. They are interested in the intersections of computer science, community organizing, gender studies, media studies, blockchain infrastructure, and other new media topics.

AR Audio Immersive Installation on the passage of time

Mercedonius is an augmented reality audio installation, coupled with mixed medium sculpture. It explores the relationship with time, space, body, and mind through different walkable scenes. The listener synchronizes, de-synchronized, and re-synchronizes themself with the environment is a dualistic dialog. They form new bonds with time apart from the clock in and clock out, and experience the world through sound to reconnect with time that has been lost. The name Mercedonius comes from the legacy month from the Roman calendar once used to manually synchronize human culture with the physical universe.
