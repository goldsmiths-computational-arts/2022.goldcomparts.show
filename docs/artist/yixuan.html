Yixuan Xu

Electronic Dreams

Yixuan Xu is a Chinese multidisciplinary artist and designer whose recent practice revolves around the intersection of art and technology in the field of computational culture, working with computer vision, artificial intelligence natural language processing, and interactive installations as her primary creative medium to understand the potential and impact of technology in a broader cultural and social context.

The project discusses the emotional and psychological relationship between humans and machines.

The project discusses the emotional and psychological relationship between humans and machines. This is based on the idea proposed by the inception of simulation (InSim) hypothesis: mental imagery and imagination are reactivated or simulated perceptions, then in order for robots to develop a strong mental imagination, it may be necessary to give them the ability to dream. The installation invites the viewer to watch the screen in the box and participate in the machine's dream description via the camera, which displays the surroundings captured by the camera in real time and gives a description which is fed into a dream text generated by machine learning and accompanied by a sound influenced by the real-time data.
