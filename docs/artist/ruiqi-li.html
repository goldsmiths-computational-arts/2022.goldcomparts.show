Ruiqi Li

Escaping the "factory"

Ruiqi Li is a Chinese-born multidisciplinary artist based between China and London, UK. She uses photography, 3D modelling, physical computing , writing and the Internet-based practices to show her examination and criticism of the comtemporary society. 3D canning and 3D installation function as a suitable activator for her practice, leading her to think about the changes in her surroundings. Lately, she combined posthuman and toy factory together to discuss the manipulation of technology and manufacturing. Ruiqi is fascinated by the collective behaviour of human beings and their cultural transmission. She tries to visualize such a phenomenon through visual art to make pose questions about our current living situations.

My work is a multimedia installation combined 3D game and interactive installation.

This is a multimedia work combining 3D visual/game/ installation/ sculpture referencing the idea of female doll factory. Inspired by Jordan wolfson’s work colored sculpture, I would like to build a installation made by a piece of screen and a set of toy hanging in steel frame which refers to mini assembly line, layers of boxes. Toys are like clones, can be copied again and again. Toys are like hosts cheering up costumers. Users can interact with the game to control the movements of the toy sculptures.I want to discuss that we post-human are all like products been packed on the assembly line and trolleys. Especially females. It is like the The mind and factory in capitalist society.
